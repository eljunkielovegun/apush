About This Study Guide

This APUSH Study Guide was created by Cuauhtemoc Patton and his father to help prepare for the AP US History exam. The content is organized according to the College Board's nine historical periods, covering the entire scope of American history from pre-Columbian societies to contemporary America.

Features of the Guide

  • Comprehensive Coverage: Detailed explanations of key events, people, and concepts for each period
  • Visual Timelines: Interactive visualizations that help you understand historical connections
  • Practice Quizzes: Self-assessment tools to test your knowledge and identify areas for further study
  • Mobile-Friendly Design: Access your study materials on any device

How to Use This Guide

The APUSH Study Guide is designed to supplement your classroom learning and textbook reading. Here are some suggested ways to make the most of these resources:

  1. Review the detailed content for each period to reinforce your understanding of key concepts
  2. Use the interactive timelines to visualize connections between events across different periods
  3. Take the practice quizzes to assess your knowledge and identify areas that need more attention
  4. Print specific sections for offline study or quick reference during review sessions

About the AP US History Exam

The AP US History exam is designed to assess your understanding of American history from approximately 1491 to the present. The exam consists of multiple-choice questions, short-answer questions, a document-based question (DBQ), and a long essay question.

The College Board organizes the course content into nine chronological periods:

  1. Period 1: 1491–1607
  2. Period 2: 1607–1754
  3. Period 3: 1754–1800
  4. Period 4: 1800–1848
  5. Period 5: 1844–1877
  6. Period 6: 1865–1898
  7. Period 7: 1890–1945
  8. Period 8: 1945–1980
  9. Period 9: 1980–Present
